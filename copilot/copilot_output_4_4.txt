**What I changed**

1. `topK()`

- Used `topk(..., dim=1)` to keep the best `beam_size` candidates per batch item.
- Recovered:
  - `top_beamid` with integer division by `vocab_size`
  - `top_wordid` with modulo by `vocab_size`

Why: this is the standard beam-search pruning step and matches the shapes expected by the rest of beam_search.py.

2. `select_hiddens_by_beams()`
- Built a gather index from `beam_id`.
- Expanded that index across all trailing dimensions.
- Used `gather(dim=1, index=...)` to reorder hidden states by selected beams.

Why: this keeps the code minimal and works both for decoder hidden states and for `model.log_prob`, which has extra trailing dimensions in beam_search.py.

3. `extract_sequences()`
- Sorted final beam scores descending with `sort(dim=1, descending=True)`.
- Backtracked from the final beam indices through `top_beamids`.
- Gathered the matching token ids from `top_wordids` at each timestep.
- Returned the reconstructed `sequences` and sorted scores.

Why: beam search stores backpointers during decoding, so reconstruction needs to traverse them backward. This is exactly what test_beam_search.py expects.
